About

I'm a first-year Robotics Engineering student at Olin College of Engineering, one of the most project-driven engineering schools in the country. I came in with four years of hands-on experience leading software teams in competitive robotics — which is really just how I learn best: build something, break it, fix it.

My background spans real-time vision systems, autonomous navigation, and scalable software pipelines. I'm especially drawn to problems where the software has to work reliably under pressure — whether that's a 2-minute FRC match or a fully autonomous UAV flight.

Outside of engineering, I'm navigating leukemia treatment while keeping active in school and side projects. It's taught me a lot about focus, resilience, and not wasting time on things that don't matter.
